Hi, I'm Julie

I'm a Full Stack Web Developer living in Atlanta, GA. I'm originally from New Orleans and attended the University of Georgia for my undergraduate degree where I majored in Psychology. Upon graduation I moved to Washington, DC and began working on Capitol Hill for David Vitter, a US Senator from Louisiana. After many years in the political realm I decided I was ready for a new challenge. I've always loved science and technology so web development was a perfect fit for my next endeavor.

I spent a year teaching myself coding through online tutorials (and a lot of trial and error!) and building websites and games for myself and friends. I realized I loved programming and wanted to pursue web development as a career. I enrolled in DigitalCrafts' full-time immersive coding bootcamp in February of 2017.

Featured Project: HuddleUp

HuddleUp is an app designed to make the little league team management process simpler. Coaches and Parents can create accounts and then join a current team, or create a team. Users can then view the team roster, send messages to other parents and view upcoming events. Coaches have access to special features like adding events and sending a text message to all parents on the team.
